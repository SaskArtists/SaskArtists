David Gilhooly (left) showing off his frogs to Elizabeth Kady and John Zeldin


David Gilhooly is a sculptor, who is known for his ceramic sculptures, most of which are of animals, food, plants, and Frogs. Or what he calls: "FrogWorld"

David was born in Auburn California in 1943, he graduated the University of California in 1967.

Later, he and 4 other artists including Robert Arneson started working together in what would be called "The Funk Ceramic Movement" in San Francisco

David moved to Regina in 1969, teaching until 1971 at the University of Saskachewan.

While still in Regina, he also was a spirtual instigator of "back to the land" alternitive media, with good humour, while David was in Regina, he worked with many artists such as Maya Peebles Bright.

David Gilhooly died on August 21, 2013 of complications related to cancer in his home.
