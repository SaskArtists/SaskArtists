F. Wayne Tunison

 

Wayne has been a bell ringer for more than four decades. He like to play bells. Wayne is the father of four children. He likes doing things with his children. Wayne sometimes plays the bells more than four times a week. He gives tours and has learned the names of many of the artists represented in the Dead Air Space Art Gallery. An average of four people come on a tour of the bell tower and some people have come up more than four times to the bell tower.

 

Wayne enjoys drawing and sometimes writes stories. Mr. Tunison teaching the bells with enjoyment. Wayne also appreciates the help he receives from the other bell ringers, Knox Metropolitan church and our neighbours from the Masonic Temple.

 

Wayne encourages the multicultural aspects of the Regina Bell Ringers along with the local composers.

Kiet Truong

 

 

Kiet was born in Cholon, Vietnam where she was a registered nurse. With the fall of Vietnam, Kiet became a small boat escapee for freedom.

 

Kiet has played the bells for more than twenty years attempting to fuse her eastern spiritualism with the modernism she embraces in her new home.

 

Kiet is the mother of one daughter.

Carol Benesh (Keelo)

 

One of the more senior apprentices, Carol has been ringing the Darke Memorial Chimes (12 English bells) since 1996. The tower bells are her first and only musical instrument (besides her voice). Carol enjoys the more volunteer aspects of belonging to the Regina Bell Ringers, like tours and mentoring the new apprentices. There are also the tasks of cataloguing the tower music and art, creating and formatting the concert programs & advertisements. Carol has reordered the accounting with the help of Knox Metropolitan professionals. Carol discovered early in life her forte of data processing. Show her a document, file, spreadsheet, database, program, picture, electronic data of any kind, tell her what you want, it becomes information, then knowledge. It is done. Time flies for her when she is on the computer as her family can well attest.

Carol has two lovely daughters, Jade and Azure, who are also involved in bellringing and are currently seeking the things in life that they love most (definitely not computer work J).

Bev Lundahl (Windy)

 

Bevs musical training got off to an interesting start when she was twelve years old. She practiced vigorously on an old pump organ at home on the farm, played her lessons for her teacher on the piano accompanied by the teachers eccentric one-eyed friend on a mandolin. In spite of such odd beginnings she obtained grade four at the Royal Conservatory of Music later playing the organ at St. Pauls United Church in Macoun for a few years in her teens. As a recreation employee at Pioneer Village in the early 1990s she was able to use her knowledge to accompany the sing-a-longs at the nursing home. She has been apprenticing on the bells for one year. Bev has two children and works in an office in Regina.

 

Mercedees Lenger (Sadie Bug)

 

Mercedees began her apprenticeship on the bells on Sunday May 9, 2010 - Mothers Day, also her seventh birthday and her Dad and new Moms anniversary. She dedicates her music to her Mommy Jody in heaven. She has been attending Knox Metropolitan United church with Grandma Bev since age three and began visiting the bell tower when she was four. Mercedees who was born in Yorkton enjoys a large extended family and especially likes her outings to the country to visit relatives in rural and northern Saskatchewan in particular her maternal Grandma Sheila in Nokomis as well as paternal Opa Lenger. at the fish camp. She sings in a junior choir and does Austrian dancing. Mercedees attends Rosemont School.
