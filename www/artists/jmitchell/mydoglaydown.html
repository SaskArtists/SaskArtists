By the flowered chair

Ginger rag mop abandoned

On the scatter rug


And I said well he was fourteen

Oldfor a cocker

And being blind like that must have been hard

Though his stump of a tail wagged merrily

When I scartched him under his chin


And I took him to be cremated

It being winter and all


No more tripping on Joe

As I thread my way to the bathroom in the dark

No more waking to squeek squeek squeek

At 4 a.m.

As he climbs onto his rocking chair


I noticed his leash on thebench in the hall

Threw it into the drawer

"Won't need this anymore"


The drawer closed with a snap like a thunderclap

And the clouds opened up

And the rain came down
