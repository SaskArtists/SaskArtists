GALLERY

Many of Robert Betteridge's paintings are paired with poems to suggest additional meaning on top of the imagery. They will be given in the descriptions of his artworks, after their titles.

Robert no longer has a studio, and as such has picked up the pen and pencil. He still makes poetry as well as art to accompany it.

Robert Betteridge Audio CD
In this 46 minute audio track, Robert details his life, his ideas on life, and experience. The art on the CD is a print of his "Mandala" artwork.
Pink Purtifications
Robert has a series of prints he calls his "Pink Purtifications," which go over the big bang, life, and armaggeddon.
After we took this picture, Robert recalled a time he had arranged mirrors in such a way that when he took a picture of himself in it using the flash, that it made him look like a mystical, spooky figure, and asked us to keep this shot in because of it.
Lark in the Meadow
Robert recollected that while he was painting, he noticed a lark had snuck in the paint.
Morning Misty Mountain
Part of a series of mountain paintings that Robert made in a time of his life where he was constantly travelling.
Microphotographs
These were not made by Robert, but rather his son, and they seem to be a source of inspiration to him. They show the chemical structure of various everyday materials.
Taj Mahal
Robert recollected that he was painting the Taj Mahal and then realized that it looked similar to the legislature buildings in Regina.
